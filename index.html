Selected case studies

Two problems, start to finish.

The short version is on the cards above. Here is how two of them actually went, from the mess at the start to the decision it changed.

Customer Lifecycle Analytics

Dell Technologies · Aug 2023 – Aug 2024
The situation

Customer data lived in three systems that did not agree (Salesforce, SAP, and Snowflake) across 12 regions. Reporting took about 9 hours, teams argued over different churn numbers, and no one could see which customers were about to leave.

What I did

I merged the three sources into one cleaned dataset, resolving 250,000+ duplicate records and standardizing region codes. I automated the reporting in Python and Tableau, ran workshops to agree on one churn definition, then trained an XGBoost model (tested against logistic regression and random forest) with SHAP and plain-language summaries so non-technical teams could use it.

The impact

Refresh time dropped from 9 hours to under 2, the churn model reached AUC 0.89, RMA turnaround fell 14 days, and the A/B testing I set up added 4,800 qualified leads. Product teams started changing warranty offers based on the model.

Reporting automation

California Secretary of State · Apr 2026 – Present
The situation

The weekly campaign-data report was fully manual: querying in Toad, structuring in Excel, sending through Outlook, with no documented handoffs, no validation, and no error handling. It took about 90 minutes every week and was easy to get wrong.

What I did

I documented the process step by step, mapped it in Microsoft Visio, and rebuilt it as an unattended Power Automate workflow with validation checks built in, so it could run without distributing incorrect data.

The impact

Report prep went from 90 minutes to under 3 minutes a week, which saves 75+ analyst hours a year, with fewer errors because the checks run automatically.

Getting four teams to agree on churn

Before building the churn model, I ran workshops with product and sales because every team defined churn differently. I got them to settle on one rule, no renewal or interaction within 90 days of contract expiry.
